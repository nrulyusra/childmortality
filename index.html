Dying Among Children Aged 5-14 Years Old

Child mortality for ages 5-14 in South, East, and Central Asia varies significantly. South Asia has higher rates due to economic and healthcare disparities. East Asia enjoys lower rates thanks to better healthcare systems. Central Asia experiences moderate rates influenced by economic transitions. Visualizing these differences underscores the need for targeted interventions to improve children's health in each region.

Child Mortality Aged 5-14

Child mortality aged 5-14 refers to the death of children within this age range due to various causes such as accidents, chronic diseases, infectious diseases, and mental health issues. Rates vary globally, with higher mortality in low-income countries due to limited access to healthcare and safety. Efforts to reduce mortality focus on improving healthcare, safety, nutrition, and education to ensure the well-being and development of these children.
